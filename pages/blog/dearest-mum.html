Dearest Mum

Dearest MUM,

Sue Scarlett Regimbald, Susie, Sue, Aunt Sue, Grammie, Wife, Mother, Mum, Ma. Sorority Sister, Social Neighbourhood watch captain , Seamstress Sewer, Sweet maker,Style dresser, Saber cat carer,Special Botanical planter, Corny Joke Sayer, Pre- School teacher Hockey mom…You put all that together what do ya get?.. Busi Sue. Self proclaimed as her Email address and license plate would suggest. Let’s Slip into it. Sue’s Sweets. A little side business she started. Anyone who knew her, knows that baking was a passion. Boy was she great at it! As far back as I can remember, Mum baked, made, created every cake for my birthday! Not just mine, but Dad’s cake, Brothers and soon commissions would come in, from the neighbours and strangers! If you were lucky enough to have one made for you, you’d know they were works of art! An Icing squeezer her paint brush, cake pans her canvas: R2 D2, hockey and football shaped with little diorama’s on top with scenes and action figures! Not only did they look incredible, but you better believe they were delicious. Mum prided herself on tasty moist baking. In her zucchini loaf, pie crust, and cookies. If you had treats elsewhere they just don’t compare, with their dryness. I missed my mums baking so much living in Vancouver that she would send me frozen banana and pumpkin loafs by mail! By the time they showed up, thawed and (Chef’s kiss!) Sue’s Sweet treats were in high demand. Paramount her chocolate brownies! The famous secret recipe! Never have their been a better brownie made before or since, known Province wide: North Bay can I get an Amen? And international, Rhode Island I’m looking at you. These were sold to coﬀee trucks across Guelph. That’s right She had to hire workers to make sure these special squares met the demands. Her employees were made up of hard working dedicated mixers, bakers and margarine container replenishers.. Who were they? You ask?.. Her 4 boys of course! We got a piece of the pie.

Mum was the most social person I’ve ever known, Her community reach far and wide. Her Pen pal in England since the late 60’s Shirley Day, Her decades long ski friend Rosemary, Lifetime Neighbours, Sorority Sisters. She Made friends with the local Disc Jockeys: Dave Hannah and Neil Clemons of 90’s C-J-O-Y. (Although I think they were in it just for the brownies she brought them come Christmas time). Friends in new places of residence: Chartwell, St Luke’s. Friends with My dad’s Ex girlfriends. Just when I thought Mum couldn’t make anymore friends, there she was in the Bank telling a new friend her life story. Here I thought it was the banking that took hrs. I guess you can chalk it up to her gift of the Gab, her ability to listen and retain, although details sometimes fuzzy. “Is this a play you’re in?. No Ma a movie, Is this a movie you’re in.? No Ma a play. Or perhaps it was simply her Love of people and time to give to strangers and family alike.

A friend of mine sent me a text the other day saying of Sue’s passing. Quote “Your Mom was one in a million. Her love and devotion to your Dad, your brothers and you was so clear.” It’s true. I’m sure you can ask my siblings how She generated Love and generosity. For Me I can count the ways. One: When I filmed the cult classic Nobody’s real, I was 13 and couldn’t drive a car, but the characters were adult detectives on a stakeout in a Van. Who’s Van? My mums forrest green ford! Mum napped in the back as us amateur filmmakers as inexperienced as they come filmed into the night! Thesame could be said for My mum being a supportive audience member, weather it was holding mass in my room, an alter boy on this very alter, an actor on stage, Mum was always watching with Love and pride in her eyes. Although she made it clear she didn’t like when I played a Bad guy..Mum Just you wait and see…

Mum always had an adventurous spirit! And We benefitted. She would provide me with such rich experiences. She seem to have a person for every occasion. I wanted to go for a plane ride? Her mechanic had a plane and I went flying. I wanted to play with an elaborate train set? She met a guy on the st and we went and conducted mini locomotions in his basement. I wanted to play with an AT AT Walker Mum would bring me to Johanne Turner’s house. She took me to my first plays: beauty and the beast, Oliver Twist! If she couldn’t provide an experience directly, she put us in scouts, she sent me to farm camp, ski school Sailing lessons, all of the things that make up who I am is because of her. Later in life when Dad wouldn’t join her for things. Mum Made it happen on her own, she came out to Vancouver despite having a walker, we got around, we explored. It didn’t end there. One of the last moments I saw My Mum on her birthday this past September. One of the first things she said without missing a beat. “What are we gonna get up to?!” We went for a long wheel around the courtyard as far as we could go!..

I’d be remiss If I didn’t mention the musicality she inspired in my life. Mum had the radio on 24 7, 45’s spinning at Christmas. Needless to say it inspired my love of oldies and rockin’ roll, and she never silenced me when I sang at the top of my lungs in my room. Us as a family were middle class but Mum made sure we never went without. We got to try every sport we wanted. Hockey stuck, and Christmases were special for all of us. Marc got his GT snow racer, Mike got his goalie pads, Matt got his Gretzky Jersey, and I got My Tom jones Tickets. My first concert! With Mum. Later I was happy to return the favour and take Mum to Neil Diamond! Last minute call to Mom, she let out one of her signature “woohoo!” And Made it to Toronto in a flash!

Some people Busy themselves with work, others with lonely pursuits. But Mum busied herself with worrying about Us, Providing a good a life for us, Documenting our lives, keeping every keepsake, every morsel of time, every photograph she took. Every hour plus conversation with a stranger at the drugstore! Every garden she planted, every sleepless night taking care of Dad and his diabetes, and brownie baking til 2am. She busied herself with Laughter. No one laughed harder at her own bad puns than herself.! Mum had so much time for others! She gave more than she took. She thought always of her grand children, her brother Mose; Her Sister Sandy, Sue’s abundant nieces and nephews, the animals and most of all her 4 boys..

One of the last things that Mum said to me as the core 4 left the room on our last visit together. She stopped me as we were leaving and mustered up the strength to say. “Eric?..” Ya ma?.. “Will you keep the 4 boys together?..”

Well Mum in the words of Elvis..

“There must be lights burning brighter somewhere.. Got to be birds flying higher in a sky more blue. If I can dream of a better land where all my brothers walk hand in hand tell me why.. oh why …oh why can’t my dream come true….!”

Lastly; If there was ever a corny joke for this moment that Mum would come up with.. I think she would say to you all… “I will never desert you!” (I hear you laughing Mum)

Say Hi to Dad for us..I will Miss & love you Forever.
